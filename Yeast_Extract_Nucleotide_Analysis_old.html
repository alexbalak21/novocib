Yeast Extracts Enriched in Nucleotides - Flavour Enhancer

Purine nucleotides (Inosine MonoPhosphate, IMP and Guanosine MonoPhosphate, GMP) are known as flavour improvers and flavour enhancers.
Since yeasts are rich in ribonucleic acid (RNA), a natural source of nucleotides (GMP, AMP, CMP and UMP), yeast extracts enriched in natural nucleotides can be used as taste enhancers.

The process of preparation of high nucleotide yeast extract consists in:

  • Heat treatment to inactivate autolytic enzymes that produce nucleosides (deprived of flavour-enhancing properties) instead of nucleotides
  • Protease treatment leading to RNA liberation
  • Phosphodiesterase treatment leading to the liberation of nucleotides
  • AMP deamination to convert AMP to IMP
    This final step is critical to increase IMP content and so increase flavour-enhancing properties of yeast extracts.
Each step can be controlled by NOVOCIB HPLC Analysis.

NOVOCIB offers analytical service of HPLC analysis of yeast extract nucleotides content. This high performance liquid chromatography analysis of nucleotides consists in:

  • Extraction of CMP, UMP, IMP, GMP and AMP by SPE (Solid Phase Extraction) procedure
  • Separation by ion-paired HPLC of 5 nucleotides (Agilent 1100)
  • NMP quantification by ion-paired UV-HPLC (peak area at 254nm).
