Totally Available Nucleotides in Human food

Nucleotides are semi-essential nutrients that have beneficial effects on Human body.

Immune System

Nucleotides contribute to strenghten the immune system by encouraging the proliferation, differenciation, and maturation of immune cells such as macrophages, lymphocytes, and neutrophiles.

Digestive System

Ingested nucleotides support trophism and enzymatic activity of intestinal mucosa.

Flavour Enhancers

Purine nucleotides (Inosine MonoPhosphate, IMP and Guanosine MonoPhosphate, GMP) are known as flavour improvers and flavour enhancers.
Since yeasts are rich in ribonucleic acid (RNA), a natural source of nucleotides (GMP, AMP, CMP and UMP), yeast extracts enriched in natural nucleotides can be used as taste enhancers.

Infant Formula Nucleotide Analysis

Nucleotides are naturally present in milk (i.e. 2-5% of the non-protein nitrogen in Human milk). Since nucleotides have positive effects in infant nutrition, manufacturers add nucleotides in baby milk powder. In a context where food additives are subjected to regulation, nucleotide additions in baby milk powders do or are about to enter under the control of strict rules. NOVOCIB analyzes CMP, UMP, GMP, IMP and AMP contents in baby milk. The analysis of other nucleotides is also available.

Contact us to know more about Infant Formula Nucleotide Analysis.


Nucleotide Level in Meat and Fish Subproducts

Immediately after death, due to respiratory arrest, ATP breakdown process starts leading to the formation of catabolite nucleotides - inosine monophosphate (IMP), inosine (Ino) and, at a later stage, hypoxanthine (Hx). Measuring nucleotide level in tissues provides an objective indicator of the freshness of fish and meat (beef, pork, lamb, poultry) subproducts. NOVOCIB's provides HPLC-based analytical service of nucleotide extraction and quantification in customer's samples. This service allows identification and validation of novel nucleotide indicators for quality control.
